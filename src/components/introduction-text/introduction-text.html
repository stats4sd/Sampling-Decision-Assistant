Introduction

The purpose of the Sampling Decision Assistant is to provide a tool to support the design of sampling strategies among UNHCR staff when there is a need for primary data collection. These operations often arise as part of needs assessment processes when not enough information is available from secondary sources. In those cases, it is important to remember that the appropriateness and feasibility of different data collection methodologies will depend on

  • The objectives of the primary data collection exercise
  • Constraints
  • The context
  • The expected benefits and costs

A careful choice of the data collection methods is important, whatever the choice, each one has advantages and disadvantages. If sampling is involved in the process, then it is important to decide whether probability based sampling is possible, which is expected to yield a sample that is representative of the population. Alternatively  the information may come from a sample drawn  using non-probability based sampling; and this choice has important consequences on how the resulting information can be used.
